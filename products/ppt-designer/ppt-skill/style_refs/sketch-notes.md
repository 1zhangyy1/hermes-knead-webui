# sketch-notes

Soft hand-drawn illustration style — warm, approachable, educational.

## Design Aesthetic

Illustration or hand-drawn feel with soft, relaxed brush strokes. Fresh, refined overall style with minimalist editorial approach. Emphasis on warmth, approachability, and friendliness while maintaining clarity. Feels like high-quality whiteboard or sketchpad work.

## Background

- Color: Warm Off-White (#FAF8F0)
- Texture: Subtle paper grain, slightly warm tone to avoid clinical feel

## Typography

### Headlines
Bold hand-drawn marker style: "thick-stroked, slightly uneven-baseline display text that reads as hand-lettered — bold marker pen quality with soft edges, like writing on a whiteboard with genuine enthusiasm." No mechanical precision.

### Body
Casual handwriting style: "clear, round-tipped script that mimics everyday note-taking — consistent sizing with slight natural variation, legible but warm, like a thoughtful person's notes."

## Visual Elements

- Connection lines with hand-drawn wavy feel, not perfectly straight
- Conceptual icons illustrating ideas rather than literal scenes
- Color fills that don't completely fill outlines — preserve hand-painted casualness
- Simple geometric shapes with rounded corners and slight wobble
- Arrows and pointers with sketchy, informal style
- Doodle-style decorative elements: small stars, spirals, underlines
- Bracket callouts and hand-circled emphasis

## Do

- Keep layouts open and well-structured despite the casual feel
- Allow imperfection — slight wobbles and uneven baselines add character
- Layer elements with subtle overlaps
- Use warm colors exclusively
- Let hand-drawn quality show in all elements, including data

## Don't

- Use perfect geometric shapes (too mechanical)
- Create photorealistic elements
- Overcrowd with too many elements
- Use pure white or cold backgrounds
- Add corporate-looking charts or tables

## Best For

Educational content, knowledge sharing, technical explanations for non-technical audiences, onboarding materials, tutorials, friendly internal presentations
