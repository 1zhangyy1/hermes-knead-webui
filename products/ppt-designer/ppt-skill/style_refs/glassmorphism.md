# glassmorphism

Frosted glass aesthetic — translucent panels, blurred depth, premium tech keynote feel.

## Design Aesthetic

Modern "frosted glass" UI aesthetic. Semi-transparent blurred panels float above colorful gradient backgrounds. Depth is created through layering translucency rather than shadows. Feels like contemporary OS design: Apple WWDC, Arc Browser, Vercel marketing. Premium, futuristic, product-centric.

## Background

- Color: Deep space gradient — indigo→violet→magenta (#1a1a4e → #6b21a8 → #be185d), or teal→navy→black (#0f766e → #1e3a8a → #000000)
- Texture: Smooth gradient; optional subtle starfield or aurora wash

## Typography

### Headlines
Semi-bold to bold geometric sans-serif: "semi-bold geometric sans-serif with clean, digital precision — contemporary tech feel, slightly wide letter-spacing. White or near-white on dark backgrounds. Think a premium SaaS product launch presentation."

### Body
Same family, regular weight: "regular-weight clean geometric sans-serif — pure white or light gray, crisp against dark backgrounds. Text sits ON the glass, never inside it. Text is NEVER blurred."

## Visual Elements

- Layered frosted-glass cards with visible blur and subtle inner highlights
- Depth via soft box shadows and stacked glass planes
- Bento-grid or asymmetric card layouts for content pages
- One hero 3D object (glass orb, refractive shape, soft isometric geometry) on cover only
- Cinematic rim lighting, volumetric glow, gentle lens bloom
- Subtle grid patterns or starfields behind the blur

## Do

- Stack glass cards with visible layering and edge highlights
- Use 3D glass/chrome objects as focal points — ONE per cover slide maximum
- Let text sit ON the glass, not inside it — maximum legibility
- Maintain translucency: background always partially visible through panels

## Don't

- Use flat design or paper texture
- Use serif display type
- Overdo 3D — one hero object maximum, never on every slide
- Let glass blur degrade text readability
- Use warm or earthy color palettes

## Best For

Tech product launches, app showcases, enterprise brand keynotes, design portfolio decks, consumer tech presentations, creative agency pitches
